Have You Ever Googled a Symptom?

Mini‑story: You feel a chest flutter. You search “chest pain young person.” The internet shows scary results. Your heart races more. Now it feels real.
Key idea: Searching can increase fear, which increases body sensations, which increases fear again.
3/21

What Is Anxiety?

The Brain–Body Alarm

  • Brain: Scans for danger
  • Body: Gets ready (heart, breath, muscles)
  • Goal: Keep you safe
Anxiety is useful. It becomes a problem when it’s constant, intense, and disrupts life.
→

Body sensations are expected during stress. They’re not dangerous by themselves.

4/21

Normal vs Problematic Anxiety

Normal

Nervous before exams, quick worry, still able to function.

Problematic

Worry sticks all day, constant checking, sleep and focus suffer.

Rule of Thumb

If anxiety regularly blocks normal life, skills/support will help.

5/21

What Is Health Anxiety?

  • Misreading normal sensations as signs of serious illness
  • Loops of checking: pulse, mirrors, devices, scans of the body
  • Reassurance (tests, forums) gives short relief → fear returns
Example: “My eyelid is twitching → must be a brain issue.” Often it’s stress, caffeine, or screen time.
Good news: Health anxiety is common and manageable. We learn to respond differently.
Helpful reframe: “This is uncomfortable, not dangerous. My alarm system is loud right now.”

Micro‑example: A health post notification spikes your heart rate. That’s the alarm, not proof of illness.
11/21

Why Googling Symptoms Backfires

  1. Search engines show rare, serious results (they’re more clicked).
  2. Reading scary stories raises anxiety → stronger body sensations.
  3. Sensations feel like “evidence,” so you search more. Loop continues.
Boundary idea: No symptom searches for 24 hours. Note how anxiety changes.

Quick Wins You Can Try Today

Box Breathing (2 mins)

Inhale 4 • Hold 4 • Exhale 4 • Hold 4 × 4 rounds
Set a 2‑min timer
Sit tall
Shoulders soft

Grounding 5‑4‑3‑2‑1

5 things you see • 4 feel • 3 hear • 2 smell • 1 taste

Helps shift attention from fear thoughts to the present.

14/21

Break the Cycle — A Simple Plan

  1. Name it: “This is my alarm system.”
  2. Slow it: 2 minutes of box breathing.
  3. Delay it: Wait 30–60 min before any checking/Googling.
  4. Do a value step: One small action that matters (study a page, call a friend, take a short walk).
15/21

Scripts You Can Use

Reframe

“Uncomfortable doesn’t mean dangerous.”

Balanced thought

“I’ve felt this before and it passed.”

Boundary with Google

“I’ll review this tomorrow, not now.”

Compassion

“This is hard—and I can handle it step by step.”

16/21

What Not To Do (For Now)

  • Don’t search symptoms repeatedly
  • Don’t measure vitals again and again
  • Don’t cancel everything because of fear
  • Do set search boundaries
  • Do limit checks to sensible times
  • Do keep living your day in small steps
17/21

Lifestyle Habits That Help

  • Sleep: Consistent schedule (aim 7–9 hrs)
  • Caffeine: Reduce/avoid during high‑anxiety days
  • Movement: Short daily walks or light exercise
  • Connection: Talk to someone you trust
  • News diet: Limit scary health content
